Palmer Jones awoke to find himself strapped to a chair.  Over his entire body was a broad square cage, and the room felt tense.  There was a smell of ozone.

Dr Schmidt was sat opposite him, outside the cage.  He was again armed with a clipboard and not one but two recording devices.  He started the tape on both of them.

"Examination one of the alien subject begins March 3rd, 1978, 1132 hours.  Subject was captured last night attempting to infiltrate Grey Base in the guise of an escaped mental patient, Palmer Jones, who had been stationed at the base to aid in our investigation of the alien structure known colloquially as the Maze.

"Subject was contained successfully and did not exhibit any unusual abilities.  Protocol is to conduct a preliminary interview before taking medical samples.

"First," he said, now addressing Palmer.  "What is your name?"

"Palmer Jones," he replied.

Dr Schmidt pressed a small button and Palmer received a sharp electric shock.  Dr Schmidt addressed the microphone.  "To induce co-operation the alien subject will be given a series of increasing electric shocks upon lying."  To Palmer."  "What is your name?"  Palmer hesitated - the shock had been painful.  Given a moment longer to think he would later have had to feel mildly guilty about the decision that he then made, but Dr Schmidt took the hesitation as a cue to shock him again.  The second shock was noticeably more painful, and Palmer opened his mouth to lie as best he could whilst informing the military of the changes he had seen within the Maze.

"We don't have names.  You could call me One."

"One?" asked Dr Schmidt in alarm.  "Are more of you coming?"

"No."

A painful shock.

"Yes.  Hundreds."

"I remind you," said Dr Schmidt.  "Any falsehood will earn you a shock."

Palmer nodded.  Schmidt's hand hovered over the button.

"I understand."  Schmidt's hand relaxed.

"Is the Maze hostile?  Was it sent by the Communists?"

"Yes, the Maze is hostile.  It is alien in origin."  No shock.

"Is there a corresponding Maze that threatens the Soviets?"

Palmer searched his memory for anything that General Morris might have said.  "No.  This Maze is unique on this planet."  No shock.

"Why has the Maze accelerated its expansion suddenly?"

Palmer leaned forwards as best he could given the restraints.  "The man Byrne allowed the Maze to experience human death for the first time, and -"

A harsh shock, longer than the others had been.  Dr Schmidt stared disapprovingly at Palmer.

"Byrne was not the first death in the Maze."  He widened his bulbous eyes as though trying to hypnotise Palmer.  "Why has the Maze accelerated its growth?"

Palmer, nonplussed, responded without thinking.  "Then I don't know."

He blacked out from the next shock.  When he came around, Dr Schmidt was repeating his question, and Palmer desperately gave the most anodyne response he could find.

"Nearing completion," he muttered, his mouth full of blood - he must have bitten his tongue, hard, during the pain.

"What does that mean?"  Schmidt was standing, now, shouting over his desk like a short order chef.  "What is completion?"

Palmer, his head swimming, said "The egg hatches."
Quick as a flash Dr Schmidt was on his feet and had pressed a button to summon his burly male orderly.  He spoke into the microphone.

"Due to alarming statements made in preliminary interview it is curtailed here.  Moving to inform high command whilst my assistant collects medical samples and neutralises the creature."  To his assistant:  "Remove its teeth."

The assistant nodded, and picked up a pair of pliers.
