"Why, howdie do, sir?" Aunt Sally curtsied at the strange figure standing motionless in the barn.

"Aunt Sally," the figure responded, coolly. "I'm Worzel."

"Worzel?" asked Aunt Sally. "Worzel don't 'ave no wooden head, sir, only ugly turnip heads." The smooth figure remained silent. "'Ere, what you done with Worzel? What you doing in his barn? And what's that thing?" She pointed at a half-finished shape.

"That, my dear Sally?" The nascent god called Worzel Gummidge smiled, a beautiful rippling motion betraying undercurrents of amusement. "Why, my newest head-that-can-carve-an incrementally-better-head. Would you like one?"
