A drabble I wrote as part of directing The Way Through The Woods for Two Shades of Blue. A memory from the perspective of the Boy who would become her Prince.

----

A young boy runs through nameless trees, their smooth trunks leaving narrow avenues down which to slip, the sanctuary of their branches just out of reach.

The raven's cry echoes in his ears, the smell of burnt flesh clings to his clothes. He stumbles over a root, and pauses to catch his breath. The Sun is hidden but lies directly ahead, Southwards, its light dully reflected by pale grey bark.

Somewhere ahead lies safety. Somewhere ahead lies the house with the stories, with the garden, the turnips, the wooden fence, with Mother. Somewhere ahead lies safety.

The boy runs onward.
