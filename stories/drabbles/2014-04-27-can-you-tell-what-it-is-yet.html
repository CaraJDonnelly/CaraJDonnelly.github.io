Miles painted, not with artistry but with care; his eye was photo-perfect. If he had been more attentive, more artistic, the town might have survived; less attentive, and their deaths would be still a mystery.

His last painting was dated as the twelfth (which, records show, was heavily overcast), and shows a beautiful young woman with full, pale lips. She lounges atop a lacquered mahogany table, gazing toward a dark window. She shines as though lit from below. Every line is perfect.

Eventually, one realises that the polished table surface lacks her reflection.
