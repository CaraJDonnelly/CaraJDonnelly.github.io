I'm new to this town
But you're newer:
You stand much too close, your fingers all clench'd
And I watch like a human reviewer.
A baby-moon face,
Honest nerves so sublime.
Tell me -- well...
...Are you just pretending
To ride this crowded Central Line?
Now the crowd of blind poets
Hears the chant it abhors:
"Please do not block the doors.
Please do not. Block. The doors."

The wheels give rise to thunder
As the silence reigns above:
Are you the the only one there
With eyes that could show love?
I know that you are frightened
And you feel you're out of place;
Flat air that's only lightened
When you leave.
Now walk faster, now you stumble
As the clockwork wonder roars:
"Please do not. Block. The doors.
Please do not. Block. The doors."

Outside the street is grimy and
The Sun lights only grey,
In your footsteps there's a struggle and
We wonder "will she stay?"
In this joyful vibrant city we need
No-one with your flaws.
"Please do not. Block. The doors.
PLEASE DO NOT.
BLOCK.
THE DOORS."
