The sail roars overhead
Screaming in the breeze
With taut iron chains
Twixt cloth and wood;
A flowing, barren frieze.

This ship has never had a crew
Nor rested in a port
But steers as though by weathervane
O'er Sea.Â  Aye, free from sense
And free from law
And free from human thought.

What cargo rides a barren ship?
What fool would seek to know?
Who'd tread on planks from Jones himself
Touch salt soaked cloth, breathe cool dead air,
Bear mortal dread to grow?

No.

No, do not board the Ozymandic Grip;
Do not chart its way.
The twisted canvas, twisted grains
Twisted screams of twisted chains
I warn you: Stay away.
