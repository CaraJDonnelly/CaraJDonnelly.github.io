Lyndsay Hailey came over to London this September as part of the iO in Europe tour, co-ordinated by the visionary Stefan Pagels Anderson.  Stefan is seeking to bring the iO philosophy - of beautiful scenes and strong connection - to European improv.  It's a noble goal.  Ten different cities in Europe were visited by teachers from iO with each visit involving workshops for the local improv community.

This intensive ran for three days, from the 22nd-24th of September, 2014, in the Nursery Theatre, with Jules Munns being utterly invaluable.  I was organising the London part of the iO in Europe tour, and I learned a lot as a result.  This page will focus on the improv teaching and shows that happened and leave the summary of 'How to Admin' for another time.

My overall impression of Lyndsay's teaching was this: She wanted us to be able to do one emotionally-connected Harold well, and she focused her energies on that goal.  With more time available there would naturally have been more material to cover, and I would be curious to know whether she would broaden her focus to give us more of a smorgasbord of improv to pick and choose from, or whether she would teach us to do one thing well, then another thing well, then... and so on.

It was a good three days.  From Sunday night when I attended the C3467X final of the 5-a-side (featuring Will Hines and Shannon O'Neill) to Wednesday night when I had a rehearsal with the musical improv troupe I'm a member of, I must have seen twelve groups perform and performed with Wolfpack with JR on Monday night.  Going back to work on Thursday was... difficult.
