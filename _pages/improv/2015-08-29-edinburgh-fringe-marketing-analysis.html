About half the game in the Edinburgh Fringe is advertising your show.  The Fringe sold more than two million tickets in 2015, and that ignores entirely the PBH Free Fringe, which itself has around 8,000 unticketed performances each year and so presumably around another quarter-million of attendance events.

Before deciding how to market your show, I ask that you sit down with your production team and ask for specific and flexible goals you would like to achieve -- for example, imagine you have a theatre with 100 seats, you might agree that you wish to sell more than 80 seats per performance at the weekend, and more than 40 seats per performance midweek.  You might also agree that you do not expect to sell more than 60 seats per performance midweek, and so if you do then either something is going surprisingly well or (more likely) everything is going okay-ish and you can ease back very slightly on the exhausting flyering.  The specifics of these goals don't matter that much; the reason for having them is that our human brains are very inconsistent in noticing problems unless we have decided in advance what 'a problem' looks like.

What follows is some basic SWOT (Strength, Weakness, Opportunity, Threat) analysis of different marketing methods one might employ in Edinburgh.  Each show is unique, and the analysis below is intended as a starting point rather than an end point.  If you think some of the methods included are silly, I invite you to consider whether following the crowd is a good way to get noticed and engage people.

I am very lazy, so my strengths and weaknesses are focused on how much day-to-day work each method takes.

Flyering

S W
  • Well-understood
  • Effect scales with size of company
  • Doesn't stand out
  • Requires discipline in cast
  • Data shows effect scales with footfall, not engagement(!)
O T
  •  Direct human contact
  • Exhaust cast, impacts show
  • Requires good organisation to not tie up all company members.

Ameliorate weaknesses and threats: teach cast rudiments of engagements, flyer in costume, flyer in busy areas, set maximum time for flyering.

Fringe Royal Mile Slots

S W
  • Defined unit of work
  • Area effect
  • Hard to engage passers-by well (needs to be a cappella singing)
  • Needs large-scale prep work with entire cast.
O T
  •  Engage large numbers of people
  •  Hurt cast voices

Ameliorate weaknesses and threats: provide water to cast, have large cast, rotate who sings with who flyers.

Print Media (e.g. The Fringe Guide, Wee Blue Book)

S W
  • Well-understood
  • Zero toil during the Fringe -- pay and design and then you're done.
  • High initial cost
  • No direct human contact
O T

Free Foot or Back Rubs

S W
  • Guaranteed engagement
  • Low effort cost
  • Novel
  • Gross
  • Doesn't scale past one or two people.
O T
  • Direct human contact
  • Hurt passer-by with bad massage
  • Be asked to move on by Fringe

Ameliorate weaknesses and threats: learn to massage properly, practise on each other.

Print Media (e.g. The Fringe Guide, Wee Blue Book)

S W
  • Well-understood
  • Zero toil
  • High initial cost
  • No direct human contact
O T

Exit polls showed print media accounted for around 30% of the 2015 run of Supermassive Face Palm's audience.

Posters

S W
  • Low toil (check they are still up)
  • Area-effect.
  • High initial cost
  • No direct human contact
  • Difficult to measure effect -- unlikely to be last interaction before someone decides to attend
O T
  • Engage people during quiet parts of day.

Portable Signage

S W
  • Visible, cheaper than paying advertising firm for fixed posters
  • Area-effect
  • Low toil (carry around large sign)
  • High initial cost
  • No direct human contact
O T
  • Damage venue by carrying around large sign.

Busking

S W
  • Strong engagement
  • Direct human contact
  • Need instruments
  • Appropriate to main show?
O T
  • Gain money from busking
  • Hurt throat singing
  • Damage instruments (e.g. rain).
  • Be asked to move along by Fringe.

Ameliorate weaknesses and threats: sing songs appropriate to main show (e.g. comedy songs), set maximum time for busking, bring covers with you.  Put out a hat.

5-minute Magic Shows

S W
  • Strong engagement
  • Direct human contact
  • Needs table
  • Scales with  number of min(magicians, tables).
O T
  • Make money
  • Practise showmanship
  • Damage or lose props
  • Sate people's desire for magic
  • Might be asked to move along

Ameliorate weaknesses and threats: don't use props from main show, tie them to table where possible.  Probably flyer rather than ask for money.

Social Media (non-automated)

S W
  • Pure engagement
  • Indirect Human contact
  • Requires long run-up in building audience.
  • You are playing someone else's game (e.g. Facebook, Edfringe) by managing your
  • following through it.
  • High time cost
  • Hard to quantify effect.
O T

Social Media (automated or paid)

S W
  • Effect scales with amount of money
  • Easy to measure effect
  • Zero toil
  • Typically low engagement.
  • No direct human contact.
O T
