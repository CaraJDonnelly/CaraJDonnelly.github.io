Morning

We will do emotion work today, and tomorrow we shall put that into a form.  We welcome J, who has joined us a day late, and Lyndsay asks five of us to do one-line summaries of their embarrassing story.  J shares with us his.

Lyndsay makes us recap yesterday's lesson, in particular our personal takes on what we learned, and uses our responses to inform what we shall be doing today.  She expounds on "Shoot The Grandma", her personal mantra for breaking out of loops: if we begin a scene where a granddaughter says to a grandmother "If you don't stop being racist, I'll shoot you", and the grandmother says "You don't have the guts", "I do have the guts", "No you don't", then we should just shoot her.  We let tension build like that sometimes, and we will focus on that today.  There are three things that -

[Suddenly a phonecall requires that I leave the intensive for work.  Damnit.]

I return around 4.30pm with half an hour of class remaining.  We are in pairs and performing scenes based on the emotions of anger, and then on the emotions of love; the words in the scenes are much less important than the emotional honesty.  I'm sad to see that I've obviously missed the most magical part of the intensive - when I come back people are...

...touching each other's shoulders in the way I would touch a friend that I had known for a reasonable length of time after that friend had had a bad (but not terrible) day.  All supportive-like.  Darn it.
